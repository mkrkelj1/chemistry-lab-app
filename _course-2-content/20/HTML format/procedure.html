Procedure for Aldol Reaction

Preparation of Aldol

Safety

1. Weigh out approximately 250 mg of solid into a vial by difference with a stir bar. In the hood, add approximately 2 mL of acetone. Begin stirring until solid is dissolved.

Wear goggles, gloves, and a lab-coat. Handle glassware with care. Glassware can break and cause injury

2. Add approximately 0.5 mL of 6 M sodium hydroxide. Add it slowly, dropwise. Continue to stir the solution until solution is uniform in color. Properly store vial in drawer for a week.

Concentrated sodium hydroxide is caustic and can cause burns. If you come in contact with NaOH, wash immediately and thoroughly with cold running water. Notify your instructor.

3. Remove vials from drawer and add 2 mL of 3 M HCl. You may have to free the solid slightly before adding the acid. Add slowly and carefully because of exothermic acid-base reaction.

HCl is corrosive and can cause burns. Wear gloves. If you come in contact with acid, wash immediately and thoroughly with cold running water.

Extraction

 

4. Carefully pour product into separatory funnel. Add approximately 5 mL of dichloromethane. Wash the vial with dichloromethane and add to the separatory funnel.

Be careful with dichloromethane, can cause skin irritation. Also, may cause cancer.

5. Wash the product with approximately 10 mL of distilled water. Remove the organic layer into separate beaker. Repeat washing with distilled water twice more and collect organic layer into same beaker.

Be sure to vent frequently while shaking separatory funnel to prevent pressure build-up. Never point funnel at yourself or another person.

6. Dry product over anhydrous Na2SO4

 

7. Decant solution into properly sized RBF, rinse beaker with dichloromethane being careful not to add too much volume to the RBF.

 

8. Distill off solvent on roto-vap

Using rotovaps can be dangerous if you do not follow the intstructions and sequences. The instrument is under vacuum, trying to remove glassware from a system under vacuum can be dangerous to yourself and others. Systems under vacuum have the potential to explode if you do not follow correct instructions.

Iso-propanol used in the cold finger is rubbing alcohol. However, while adding dry ice into the alcohol spillage occurs if you add huge amounts in one addition. Dry ice can cause frostbite. Wear oven mitts while handling dry ice.

Purification

 

9. Recrystallize the aldol using distilled water. Heat the solvent using a heating mantle. Filter using vacuum filtration, rinse once with ice cold solvent.

Hot plate is HOT. Wear oven mitts while handling hot plate and hot glassware.

10.  Weigh final product and store in glass vial.
