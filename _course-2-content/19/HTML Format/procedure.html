Procedure

Preparation of Ester

Safety Precautions

1. An oven-dried round-bottom flask is obtained. 64 mL of 1-butanol and 50 mL of acetic acid are added to the RBF.

Wear goggles, gloves, and lab-coat. Work in fume hood.

Handle glass-ware with care.

Carboxylic acids are corrosive and can cause burns. If you have contact with carboxylic acids, rinse off skin immediately.

2. Approximately 10 mL of concentrated sulfuric acid is added carefully and slowly.

Concentrated sulfuric acid is very corrosive. Wear thick gloves while handling. If you have contact with it, wash immediately and thoroughly with cold running water. Notify the instructors while you continue to wash.

3. A heating mantle is set-up below the RBF. A reflux condenser is placed on the round-bottom flask and reaction mixture refluxed for 3h. After 3h, the RBF is cooled without removing condenser. The condense is removed after the reaction mixture is cooled to room temperature.

Handle glass-ware with care. Glassware can break and can cause injury. Hot plate used for reflux is HOT. Wear oven mitts with handling hot plates. HOT hot plate can cause burns. Make sure cooling water inlet and outlet are securely fastened onto the condenser. If you have an injury or burn, immediately notify your instructors.

4. The reaction mixture is transferred into a separatory funnel. The flask is rinsed with distilled waters several times and the rinse is added to the funnel. Water (50 mL) is added to the funnel. The separatory funnel is shaken gently with frequent venting for several minutes. Drain aqueous layer into Erlenmeyer flask and discard. Repeat washing of organic layer with water.

While performing an extraction with a separatory funnel, do not point the stem of the funnel toward anyone, including you. Pressure can build up in the separatory funnel while you shake, the glass stopper can fly off from the top and injure you or your hood-mate. Liquids inside the funnel can leak while you shake. To avoid all of the above, frequent venting is a MUST.

Extraction

 

5. A saturated solution of sodium bicarbonate is prepared. 50 mL of the NaHCO3 is added to the separatory funnel. The funnel is shaken and vented frequently until gas evolution stops. The aqueous layer is removed. The process is repeated.

CO2 evolution occurs. Do not inhale the gas.

Frequent venting of the separatory funnel is OBLIGATORY as the pressure builds-up due to CO2 evolution.

6. The organic layer is removed from the separatory funnel and dried over anhydrous CaCl2.

None.

Purification of Crude Ester

 

7. Crude ester is purified by distillation.

Handle glass-ware with care. Glassware can break and can cause injury. Hot plate used for reflux is HOT. Wear oven mitts with handling hot plates. HOT hot plate can cause burns. Make sure cooling water inlet and outlet are securely fastened onto the condenser. If you have an injury or burn, immediately notify your instructors.

Characterization of Product

 

8. The product is characterized by BP, IR, and NMR spectroscopy.

IR cells are breakable. Wash the cells in the fume-hood using methylene chloride and hexanes. Methylene chloride is suspected of causing cancer. Avoid exposure and inhalation of solvents used for washing cells.

9. The percent purity of the product and mass spectrum obtained via GC-MS.

Your sample for GC-MS is dissolved in methylene chloride. Sample solution must be made in the fume-hood. Avoid exposure and inhalation to solvents and synthesized esters.
